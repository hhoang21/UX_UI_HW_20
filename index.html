Paw Buddy

To help users who loves dogs but struggle to find the companionship and or emotional support dogs can provide

My Team

Team

Tools

Figma, Miro

Timeline

3 Weeks

My Role

In this project my role was as a reseacher and designer. I participated in every step of the way but individually, I designed the checkout wireframes and verifying ID wireframes.

Defining the Challenge

Between the members of our team, only one of us owned a pet. We wanted to examine why so many people don’t own dogs and how we can come up with a solution to their pain points.


Goal

We wanted to design a digital way for users to borrow dogs without the burdens of owning a dog.

Research

Method

To narrow down the most prominent issues, we conducted 5 interviews and around 20 surveys.

Findings

After thoroughly analyzing the interviews and surveys, it was concluded that most users are not able to own dogs because of financial burdens or they don’t have the time.

Problem Statement

A cynophilist, better known as one who loves dogs, struggles to find the companionship and or emotional support dogs can provide, simply because they do not have the bandwidth or perhaps even the means to take on the responsibility of owning and caring for the dog full time.

Prototyping

Before we began our prototype, we created a user flow to map out how Leanne would use each feature of our app. Our first user flow was rough and so with helpful feedback, we were able to create a new one that flowed much more with our imagined design. Because we each divided a section of the app for us to complete and we all had a rough idea of what we wanted our app to look like, we all started at the low fidelity level rather than paper prototype. After our low fidelity, we incorporated more of our style and precision into the prototype to create our mid-fidelity.

User Testing

We had a total of three users test our mid-fidelity for user errors in our design.



Findings

During our user testings, we found that some of our buttons were not clickable due to an error when we were connecting each button. We also noticed that users need to input their Id in before checkout for a much faster checkout.

Final Design

We created a final design where we were very proud of! Although rough as it is, we were overjoyed and proud of our work on our first prototype.

Final Thoughts

Since this was my first time working in a group for a project in UX/UI design, it was a challenge, however I learned to to more open to ideas and explore other ideas instead of locking myself in my own box.
